Where Culture Meets Code

About

I'm Natasha Mboya, a creative technologist and software engineer currently transitioning into art school. I work at the intersection of design and technology, where code, systems, and visual form become tools for storytelling.

With roots in Kenya, I'm interested in how traditional cultural elements can be reinterpreted through contemporary digital media. My work often blends fashion, interactive art, and creative coding to explore identity, memory, and connection—treating technology not just as a functional tool, but as a cultural medium.

Practice

My practice centers on building modular, interactive systems that invite exploration and reflection. I'm drawn to projects that reframe familiar concepts—such as time, movement, or the body—through speculative and experiential design.

Across my work, I focus on clarity of form, reusability, and strong visual language, whether I'm creating generative artwork, immersive environments, or interactive web-based experiences. I approach both art and software as systems that can evolve, recombine, and respond to human action.

Studio & Collaboration

Alongside my sister, I'm a co-founder of STUDIO, a creative studio conceived as a portal to immersive, boundary-pushing worlds. Founded at the intersection of performance, digital art, and world-building, MBOSS creates surprising, subversive, and deeply experiential works that challenge perception and expand possibility.

Collaboration is central to my practice, and STUDIO serves as a space to explore ideas that move fluidly between the digital and physical.

Background

Before moving more fully into art and experimental work, I spent several years as a full-stack software engineer, specializing in data, visualization, and frontend UX. This technical foundation continues to shape how I approach creative projects—bringing structure, scalability, and systems thinking into my artistic practice.
