Experimental Clock

Description

An experimental clock that measures cultural continuity instead of hours by visualizing the projected lifespans of Kenyan tribes and their languages. A single clock face compresses 120 years into one minute, with each tribe represented as a breathing petal that expands or contracts based on years remaining. Color encodes vitality—from flourishing to extinct—and petals turn red and disappear as extinction approaches.

By looping a century every minute, the clock reframes time as identity, memory, and preservation rather than a neutral, linear measure. It reflects how cultural continuity can be lost or reclaimed through human action, inviting viewers to see time as collective, elastic, and something we can actively steward.
